Look in School Libraries How to look for books in our school library.

Decide how you want to look for books:

Do you want to look by title?

By subject?

By author?

By series?

By all of the above? Then do a keyword search.

Write what you're looking for in the space bar.

Press the keyword, the title, the subject, the author or the series button.

Then you'll see a list of books that may interest you.

Under each book, you'll see its Dewey number. Make a note of this.

When you come to the library to pull the book from the shelf, you'll need to know its Dewey number.

To the right of each book, you'll see how many copies of the book the library owns, and how many are on the shelves now.

For example, '2 of 3' available means that the library has 3 copies of the book, and 2 copies are on the shelves right now.

How to look for books in other school libraries.

You do this in the same way that you look for books in our school library, except for one thing.

Find the little box that says 'Location' and click on the tiny arrow to the right.

Click on Miami-Dade County Public Schools.

Then do your search.

If you find a book that you'd like to order, you can't order it yourself. Ask the Fienberg-Fisher librarian to order the book for you. It will probably take a week for us to receive the book.

Look in the Public Library Why use the public library?

  • Our school library has 11,000 books....but the Miami Beach Public Library has 25,000 books for kids and teenagers.

  • Miami-Dade County Public Schools has thousands of books... but the public library has three and a half million.

  • At school, you can check out two books at a time. At the public library, you can check out up to 50 books at a time.

  • At school, kids can only check out books. The public library lets you check out books, videos and audio books.

  • As long as you return library materials on time, the public library is free.

  • You can order materials online from any of the 47 libraries in the public library system. You pick them up from the public library nearest you.

  • You can renew materials online, too.

How to look for materials in the public library

The easiest way to look for materials is by doing a keyword search. Just type in any word(s) connected with the book.

You might type in the name of the book. You don't have to write all of the words in the name. For example, instead of writing 'Captain Underpants and the Perilous Plot of Professor Poopypants' you could write 'Underpants Perilous', or maybe just 'Poopypants'.

Or you might type in what the book is about. For example, you might write 'detectives', 'dinosaurs' or George Washington.

You might type the author of the book.

Or the series that the book belongs to. For example, 'Series of Unfortunate Events' or 'Hardy Boys'.

Then just press the 'Go' button'.

How to understand the results list

Under each title, you'll see how many copies are in the Miami-Dade Public Library system.

You'll also see how many people have asked to check out copies.

For example, if you see

My Life at Fienberg-Fisher

Copies:10

Requests: 5

that means that there are 10 copies of this book in the public library system, and that if you decide to ask a librarian to send you

this book, you'll be the 6th person in line. You should get this book in 3 or 4 days, because there are plenty of books to go around.

If, however, you see

The Vampire's Kiss

Copies: 50

Requests: 468

you may have to wait several weeks until you can get a copy of this book, because so many people want it.

You can find out more about any title by clicking on it. Then, you'll see which public libraries have a copy on their shelves, and maybe you'll find a review of this book as well.

You can access many online articles through the databases available at the Miami-Dade Public Library System. From the homepage at www.mdpls.org, click on Databases & More on the left-hand side of the screen. You will then be prompted to type in your library card number and PIN. Once you do that, a list of all of our databases will come up. You can then click on subject headings such as Teen Resources or Magazines & Newspapers to find online articles. Most of the academic articles can be found by doing a search with one of our more general databases, such as General OneFile, Gale PowerSearch and SIRS Researcher. There is also a database called Opposing Viewpoints which provides a comprehensive selection of articles, essays and multimedia information about many current or controversial topics. You can also check the Florida Electronic Library at www.flelibrary.org for more articles/periodicals too
